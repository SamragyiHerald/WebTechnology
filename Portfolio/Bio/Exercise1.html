IBM Founder Thomas J. Watson Sr

Thomas John Watson Sr., (born and died in February 17,1874-June 19,1956) was an American businessman and the CEO and chairman of IBM.

  • Early Life

    • Thomas J Watson was born on February 17, 1874 in Campbell, New York. His father owned a farm which was then looked over by Watson. Watson first worked as a teacher but after his first day as a teacher, he left the profession and got involved in Accounting and Business school. Watson then, started solding sewing machines and piano.Watson also worked as an accontant, direct-sales representatives, seller of piano and more. Later, Watson found himself persistent with his job as a businessman which later helped him with his life as a famous businessman.

  • Personal Life

    • Thomas Watson got married to Jeanette Kittredge on April 17,1913. Watson and his wife Jeanette had two sons and two daughters. Their son Thomas Watson Jr., served as an ambassador to the Soviet Union under Jimmy Carter. Jeanette Watson Irwin, daughter of Watson and Jeanette got married to the late ambassador to France, John N.Irwin II. Similarly, daughter Helen Watson Buckner became a New York City based philanthropist. And son Arthur K.Watson was a presdient of IBM World Trade Corporation and also the ambassador to France.
